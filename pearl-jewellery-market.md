<p><p>The pearl jewellery market is primarily divided into two main types: saltwater and freshwater pearls. Saltwater pearls are produced in oysters found in oceanic waters, typically known for their luster and round shape, making them highly sought after. In contrast, freshwater pearls are cultivated in mussels within freshwater lakes and rivers, offering a wider variety of shapes and colors, often at lower prices. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, influencing their unique positioning in the jewellery market.</p></p>
