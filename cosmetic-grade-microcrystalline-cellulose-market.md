<p><p>The cosmetic grade microcrystalline cellulose market is primarily segmented into two types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based. Wood pulp-based microcrystalline cellulose is derived from plant fibers, offering a cost-effective and versatile option for formulations, often used as a thickener or stabilizer. Refined cotton-based microcrystalline cellulose, on the other hand, is sourced from high-quality cotton, providing superior purity and enhanced functionality in cosmetics. Each type serves specific needs in the cosmetic industry, influencing texture, stability, and performance in products.</p></p>
