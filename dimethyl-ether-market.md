<p><ul><li>Direct Synthesis</li><li>Indirect Synthesis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Dimethyl Ether (DME) market is primarily categorized into two types based on synthesis methods: direct synthesis and indirect synthesis. Direct synthesis involves the conversion of syngas (a mixture of hydrogen and carbon monoxide) into DME in a single step, resulting in higher efficiency and lower costs. In contrast, indirect synthesis entails producing DME through methanol as an intermediate, which can lead to increased complexity and longer production times. Both methods serve to meet the growing demand for renewable energy sources and cleaner fuels.</p></p>
