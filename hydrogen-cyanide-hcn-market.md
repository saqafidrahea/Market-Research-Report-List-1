<p><p>Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN) is produced through various processes, with the most notable being the Andrussow and Acrylonitrile methods. The Andrussow process involves the reaction of methane, ammonia, and air, resulting in efficient HCN production. The Acrylonitrile process, on the other hand, synthesizes HCN as a byproduct in the manufacturing of acrylonitrile. Other methods include the formamide decomposition and sodium cyanide hydrolysis processes. These diverse production techniques cater to varying industrial applications and market demands for HCN.</p></p>
